How I came to programming

(For anyone who cares)

I developed my passion for programming early - I was somewhere around 8 years old when my father suggested trying out the graphical programming language Scratch for fun. It got me hooked immediately, but after 3 years I switched to Python, as I was annoyed with the limitations of Scratch. I did some small things with it, for example a very basic text based RPG and a chatbot with fixed responses.

Next, I discovered web development - primarily JavaScript, because programming was still what I liked the most. But as with Python, the biggest website I made contained nothing but an ancient-looking calculator.

Then I switched to Java. This is the programming language I used for my more notable projects. The ones which I am most proud of are Synthesizer, LaunchpadInterface and LaunchpadAPI, a newer, better, more flexible and cooler version of LaunchpadInterface.
